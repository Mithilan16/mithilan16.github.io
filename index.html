ARAIG Flight Simulation Shell

ARAIG (As Real As It Gets) is a commercial multi-sensory suit intended to enhance virtual reality experiences with force and audio feedback making you feel as if you were actually there! ARAIG has many applications that range from gaming and movies to training for various dangerous or strenuous environments.

Dr.Coleshill, our 3rd semester object-oriented software development professor, had been working on a cross-disciplinary applied research project between Seneca's School of Aviation and School of Information Communications Technology that was intended to enhance flight stimulation training. In light of this, we were given the opportunity to write ARAIG evaluation control software, as a final project. We were tasked with creating a C++ based object oriented console application that emulated loading and executing flight stimulation data on the suit. The project involved the use of important C++ concepts such as inheritance, polymorphism, and heavy use of the STL library (unique/shared pointers, vectors, lists). This was a great learning experience which allowed us to use knowledge learned in the class room to devise, design and implement software that has real world implications.
